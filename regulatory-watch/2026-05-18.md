### ⚠️ URGENT — Complementary Law 227/2026 imposes ITCMD on offshore trusts and foreign assets
**What changed:** Enacted January 13, 2026, Complementary Law 227/2026 requires all Brazilian states to adopt progressive ITCMD (inheritance and gift tax) rates up to 8%, closes the loophole for untaxed foreign-sited assets, and introduces the first national rules for offshore trust taxation. Distributions from foreign trusts to Brazilian beneficiaries are now taxable ITCMD events. Combined with Lei 14.754/2023's 15% annual income tax on trust income, offshore trusts now face a double-taxation risk. States must adapt their legislation in 2026.
**What it means for clients:** Brazilian-connected clients with offshore trusts face a new inheritance/gift tax of up to 8% on trust distributions, stacked on top of the 15% annual income tax on trust income under Lei 14.754/2023; existing offshore trust structures serving Brazilian beneficiaries require urgent review and likely restructuring before distributions are made or states finalize implementing rules.

**What changed:** The OECD released its Side-by-Side Package on Pillar Two in February 2026, providing comparative guidance for jurisdictions implementing either a QDMTT or an IIR. The package introduces a new Simplified ETR Safe Harbor aimed at reducing compliance burdens for groups temporarily falling outside other safe harbors. Further simplification of reporting obligations is under consideration.
**What it means for clients:** Multinational clients with operations across multiple Pillar Two jurisdictions can use the Side-by-Side Package to map their QDMTT vs. IIR exposure and assess eligibility for the new Simplified ETR Safe Harbor, potentially reducing compliance costs where other safe harbors are unavailable.
