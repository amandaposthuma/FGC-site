## Brazil

### 👁 WATCH — Complementary Law 227/2026: Major ITCMD Inheritance Tax Reform
**What changed:** Enacted January 13, 2026, LC 227/2026 mandates progressive ITCMD rates across all states (max 8% in brackets based on market value at date of transfer), ends the constitutional gap on foreign assets held by Brazilian residents, and expressly subjects trust distributions and deemed transfers to ITCMD. PGBL private pension distributions are fully subject to ITCMD; VGBL only for contributions made within five years prior to death of the settlor.
**What it means for clients:** Brazilian resident HNW clients with foreign trusts, PGBL pensions, or assets held abroad now face ITCMD exposure at progressive rates up to 8%; every trust distribution is now a potentially taxable event, and PGBL balances transferred at death are fully subject to ITCMD regardless of contribution date. Clients with VGBL policies should urgently audit contributions made within the past five years for potential estate tax exposure.

### 👁 WATCH — One Big Beautiful Bill Act: Federal Estate Tax Exemption Permanently Set at $15M
**What changed:** Signed July 4, 2025, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act permanently raises the federal unified estate and gift tax exemption to $15 million per individual ($30 million for married couples) effective January 1, 2026, indexed for inflation thereafter. No sunset provision applies. The top marginal rate remains 40%. Annual gift tax exclusion remains $19,000 per recipient in 2026.
**What it means for clients:** US HNW clients and non-residents with US-sited assets can now plan around a permanent $15M individual exemption — eliminating the prior sunset risk and creating significant front-loaded gifting and trust-transfer opportunities. ## Cyprus

### 👁 WATCH — Cyprus Comprehensive Tax Reform Effective January 1, 2026
**What changed:** Cyprus parliament approved a sweeping tax reform on December 22, 2025, effective January 1, 2026. Key changes: (1) All Cyprus-registered companies are now automatically tax resident regardless of management and control location; (2) Deemed Dividend Distribution rules abolished for post-2025 profits; (3) SDC rate on actual dividends from post-2026 profits reduced from 17% to 5%; (4) All individuals aged 25+ must file annual tax returns; (5) Tax authorities gain expanded powers to request banking information covering six years.
**What it means for clients:** The elimination of the management-and-control tax residency test for Cyprus companies fundamentally changes the planning landscape — clients who relied on Cyprus holding companies managed from abroad for non-Cyprus-resident treatment now have Cyprus-resident companies. Separately, the SDC rate reduction from 17% to 5% on actual dividends from 2026 profits creates a significant distribution planning opportunity, while expanded tax authority powers to access six years of banking information increases transparency risk for clients with Cyprus structures.
**Source:** Harneys — 2026-01-01 — https://www.harneys.com/our-blogs/regulatory/a-new-era-for-cyprus-taxation-the-key-insights-of-the-2026-cyprus-tax-reform/

## Bahamas

### 👁 WATCH — Bahamas Usufruct Interest Act 2026 Enacted March 23, 2026
**What changed:** The Bahamas enacted the Usufruct Interest Act, 2026 on March 23, 2026, establishing the first comprehensive statutory framework for usufructs in the jurisdiction. The Act enables separation of the right to use/enjoy/receive income from an asset from its legal ownership. Covers equity interests, securities, IP rights, digital assets, receivables, insurance policy rights, and real/personal property. Usufructs may last up to 99 years for natural persons and 30 years for legal entities. Digital assets are expressly included.
**What it means for clients:** The Bahamas Usufruct Interest Act creates a powerful new succession planning tool for HNW clients: income or use rights over equity stakes, digital assets, and real property can now be carved off from legal ownership for up to 99 years. This is particularly valuable for clients with significant crypto holdings who want to transfer legal ownership to heirs while retaining income rights — a structure previously unavailable in most offshore jurisdictions.
**Source:** Bahamas Financial Services Board — 2026-05-01 — https://bfsb-bahamas.com/blog/2026/05/structures-in-practice-a-new-structuring-tool-usufruct-interests-in-the-bahamas/
