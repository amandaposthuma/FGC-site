## BVI

### ⚠️ URGENT — BVI FSC full enforcement of beneficial ownership regime from April 1, 2026; escalating penalties and strike-off risk
**What changed:** The BVI FSC entered full enforcement mode on April 1, 2026. Entities that missed the January 1, 2026 beneficial ownership filing deadline are accruing penalties of $600 per entity for the first three months (April–June 2026), rising to $800 for the following three months (July–September 2026), with strike-off possible after six months of non-compliance (approximately October 2026). **What it means for clients:** Clients holding BVI entities that have not yet filed beneficial ownership information are already incurring monetary penalties and face potential strike-off of their vehicle—and loss of the underlying structure or assets—from approximately October 2026. **What changed:** Beneficial ownership data in the BVI statutory register is now accessible from April 2026 to 'obliged entities' (such as registered agents and financial institutions) and to individuals demonstrating 'legitimate interest' (25%+ direct or indirect ownership or equivalent control). **What it means for clients:** HNW clients using BVI entities should be aware that their ownership information — if filed — is now visible to counterparty banks, registered agents, and others who can establish legitimate interest. Foreign investment income is taxable only if remitted to Malta, subject to a minimum €5,000 annual charge and a 15% flat rate under the Resident Non-Dom scheme. Capital gains on securities remain exempt.
**What it means for clients:** HNW clients considering a change of domicile or an alternative European base for family wealth management should note Malta's zero-inheritance, zero-estate-tax environment and comparatively low flat rate on remitted income. Comprehensive planning — including domicile analysis and remittance strategy — is required before relying on the regime.
**Source:** ImmigrantInvest — 2026-01-15 — https://immigrantinvest.com/blog/malta-non-dom-regime/
