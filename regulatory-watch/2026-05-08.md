## SINGAPORE

### URGENT — Corporate governance amendments effective 6 May 2026
**What changed:** Key provisions of Singapore's Corporate and Accounting Laws (Amendment) Act 2025 commenced on 6 May 2026. Directors are now immediately disqualified upon conviction of money laundering offences under the Corruption, Drug Trafficking and Other Serious Crimes Act. Maximum fines for directors breaching statutory duties have increased fourfold, from S$5,000 to S$20,000. The Act also introduces mandatory named engagement partners for audit transparency.
**Why it matters:** FGC clients with Singapore-incorporated holding companies or family offices must review their directorship structures to confirm no director faces exposure under the expanded AML disqualification criteria; breaches now carry materially higher penalties effective immediately.
**Source:** [ACRA — Commencement of Key Changes, 6 May 2026](https://www.acra.gov.sg/news-events/news-announcements/commencement-of-key-changes-under-the-corporate-and-accounting-laws-amendment-act-2025/); [Dentons Rodyk — 6 May 2026](https://dentons.rodyk.com/en/insights/alerts/2026/may/6/key-legislative-changes-brought-in-by-the-corporate-and-accounting-laws-amendment-act-2025)

### URGENT — Pillar Two GIR registration portal open; 30 June 2026 filing deadline
**What changed:** IRAS opened its registration portal for Pillar Two Multinational Enterprise Top-up Tax (MTT) and Domestic Top-up Tax (DTT) in May 2026. In-scope MNE groups (consolidated revenue ≥ €750M) whose first financial year under the MMT Act ended 31 December 2025 must register and file their GloBE Information Return (GIR) by 30 June 2026. Late registration carries a 10% surcharge on any MTT or DTT owed.
**Why it matters:** FGC clients with Singapore-parented or Singapore-constituent MNE group structures have 53 days to register and confirm GIR readiness; advisors should simultaneously verify whether Singapore's QDMTT safe harbor applies to reduce exposure under the income inclusion rule.
**Source:** [RSM Singapore — Pillar Two Registration Starts May 2026](https://www.rsm.global/singapore/insights/singapore-beps-20-pillar-two-update-registration-starts-may-2026); [IRAS — Registration Portal](https://www.iras.gov.sg/taxes/pillar-2-top-up-taxes/registration-for-multinational-enterprise-top-up-tax-and-domestic-top-up-tax); [EY Singapore Tax Alert — 2026](https://taxnews.ey.com/news/2026-0226-oecd-releases-side-by-side-package-on-pillar-two-global-minimum-tax-detailed-review)

## UNITED STATES

### WATCH — FinCEN proposes fundamental reform of AML/CFT program requirements
**What changed:** On 7 April 2026, FinCEN published an NPRM in the Federal Register to fundamentally overhaul how U.S. financial institutions design AML/CFT compliance programs. The proposed rule introduces a two-tiered "establishment vs. implementation" framework, codifies mandatory risk-assessment processes, requires designation of a U.S.-based compliance officer, and allows federal banking supervisors to give FinCEN 30 days' advance notice before significant AML supervisory actions. Public comment period closes 9 June 2026, with a proposed 12-month implementation period after a final rule is issued.
**Why it matters:** The rule will reshape compliance requirements for U.S.-based financial intermediaries used by FGC clients — including trust companies, broker-dealers, and RIAs — and may increase due diligence scrutiny of accounts linked to offshore holding structures and foreign-owned entities. Comments from the industry could shape the final rule materially.
**Source:** [Federal Register, 10 April 2026](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/10/2026-07033/anti-money-laundering-and-countering-the-financing-of-terrorism-programs); [Mayer Brown — April 2026](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2026/04/out-with-the-old-in-with-the-risk-based-fincen-proposes-fundamental-reform-of-aml-cft-program-requirements); [Jones Day — May 2026](https://www.jonesday.com/en/insights/2026/05/modernizing-amlcft-fincen-proposes-fundamental-reforms-to-program-requirements)

## BRITISH VIRGIN ISLANDS

### WATCH — Legitimate Interest Access to beneficial ownership register now fully operational
**What changed:** The BVI's Legitimate Interest Access (LIA) regime became fully operational on 1 April 2026. Financial institutions, law firms, journalists, and civil society organisations may now submit formal requests to the Registrar for beneficial ownership information on BVI entities. Information disclosed is limited to individuals holding ≥25% ownership or equivalent control, and covers full legal name, nationality, month/year of birth, and nature of interest.
**Why it matters:** FGC clients relying on BVI holding structures should treat the BO register as effectively semi-public for regulated third parties. Clients who have not yet filed an exemption application (available since 2 January 2026) — available on grounds including personal safety risk or minor status — should consider doing so urgently. Inaccurate BO filings now create direct legal exposure if queried.
**Source:** [Harneys — BVI LIA now operational](https://www.harneys.com/our-blogs/regulatory/bvi-launches-new-beneficial-ownership-access-policy/); [Maples Group — April 2026](https://maples.com/regulatory-round-up/bvi-beneficial-ownership-legitimate-interest-access-now-fully-operational); [Appleby — Legitimate Interest Access](https://www.applebyglobal.com/publications/navigating-the-new-legitimate-interest-access-regime-for-bvi-beneficial-ownership-information/)

## CAYMAN ISLANDS

### FYI — Tokenised fund regulatory framework now in force
**What changed:** The Mutual Funds (Amendment) Act, 2026 and the Private Funds (Amendment) Act, 2026 came into force on 24 March 2026, establishing CIMA's framework for tokenised mutual funds and tokenised private funds. Tokenised funds are classified and regulated identically to traditional funds but must satisfy additional requirements tied to the use of digital equity or investment tokens.
**Why it matters:** Wealth management clients or sponsors evaluating tokenised fund structures in Cayman now have regulatory clarity; fund managers should assess whether any existing or planned structures involve digital tokens that trigger the new requirements.

**What changed:** The eighth amendment to the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984 came into force on 20 March 2026. ## BRAZIL

### FYI — 10% withholding tax on dividends to non-residents fully in effect
**What changed:** Law 15,270/2025 (effective 1 January 2026) reintroduced Brazilian withholding income tax on dividends and profits distributed to non-resident individuals and legal entities at a flat 10% rate, regardless of amount. For resident individuals, a 10% WHT applies to monthly dividends exceeding BRL 50,000 from the same payer. Transitional relief applies only to profits formally approved for distribution before December 2025.
**Why it matters:** FGC clients with Brazilian operating companies or investment structures paying dividends offshore are now incurring withholding costs; existing holding company structures (including BVI and Cayman SPVs above Brazilian subsidiaries) should be reviewed for treaty relief eligibility under Brazil's applicable double tax treaties and Receita Federal compliance obligations.
**Source:** [PwC — Brazil dividend withholding tax](https://www.pwc.com/us/en/services/tax/library/brazil-introduces-new-dividend-withholding-income-tax.html); [Trench Rossi Watanabe — November 2025](https://www.trenchrossi.com/en/legal-alerts/brazil-enacts-law-15270-2025-which-taxes-dividends-and-amend-personal-income-tax-rules/); [Felsberg — Law 15,270/25](https://www.felsberg.com.br/en/law-15270-taxation-profits-dividends-2026/)
