## International (OECD)

### ⚠️ URGENT — OECD Pillar Two GIR central filing deadline: June 30, 2026
**What changed:** The OECD released a Common Understanding on GIR Central Filing with 33 jurisdictions confirmed as ready for central filing. Calendar-year taxpayers benefit from an 18-month first-year extension making June 30, 2026 the first filing deadline for Global Information Returns. Jurisdictions may waive local filing penalties where a GIR is centrally filed in one of the listed jurisdictions.
**What it means for clients:** MNE clients subject to Pillar Two with calendar-year accounting periods must file or ensure their GIR is filed centrally by June 30, 2026 — missing this deadline risks local filing penalties in each jurisdiction where the group operates. **What changed:** On January 5, 2026, the OECD Inclusive Framework agreed the Pillar Two Side-by-Side Package via new Administrative Guidance, including four new safe harbors. **What it means for clients:** MNE clients headquartered in a jurisdiction with an eligible domestic tax regime may qualify for the Side-by-Side Safe Harbour, potentially eliminating top-up tax exposure in other jurisdictions; US-headquartered groups are fully exempt from Pillar Two, which is directly relevant to any clients considering group restructuring decisions based on Pillar Two exposure.

**What changed:** Enacted January 13, 2026, LC 227/2026 mandates all Brazilian states adopt progressive ITCMD (inheritance and gift tax) rates up to a 16% federal ceiling. The law closes a longstanding loophole by authorizing taxation of inheritances and donations involving assets located abroad held by Brazilian residents, and requires equity to be valued at market value rather than net book value. Major changes, including the foreign asset provisions, are broadly effective January 1, 2027.
**What it means for clients:** Brazilian-resident HNW clients with offshore trusts, foreign foundations, or overseas investment accounts will for the first time face state-level ITCMD on gifts and inheritances involving those foreign assets from January 1, 2027; succession planning structures should be reviewed before year-end to assess exposure and identify restructuring options, which can take months to implement.

**What it means for clients:** Brazilian-resident clients using stablecoins for cross-border transfers are now subject to FX reporting rules; transactions above applicable limits may be restricted and each transaction must be reported to the BCB with counterparty identification, creating a paper trail with direct tax and exchange-control compliance implications.

## Cyprus

### 👁 WATCH — Cyprus comprehensive tax reform effective January 1, 2026: CIT raised to 15%, DDD abolished, dividend WHT cut to 5%
**What changed:** Cyprus enacted a comprehensive tax reform effective January 1, 2026. Corporate Income Tax increased from 12.5% to 15% to align with OECD Pillar Two. **What it means for clients:** Clients using Cyprus holding companies or fund structures face a higher headline CIT rate but benefit materially from DDD abolition — eliminating prior annual deemed distribution charges — and the reduced 5% dividend WHT; the overall tax efficiency of Cyprus structures should be remodelled under the new regime to determine whether existing structuring assumptions remain valid.
