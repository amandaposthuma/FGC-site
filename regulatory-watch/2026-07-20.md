## Brazil

### 👁 WATCH — Brazil double-layer taxation on foreign trusts: 15% CFC income tax (Lei 14.754/2023) + up to 8% ITCMD (LC 227/2026) now fully operative
**What changed:** Brazilian residents holding assets through foreign trusts face two layers of Brazilian taxation in 2026: a 15% flat federal income tax on annual trust earnings under Lei 14.754/2023 (regardless of distributions), and state-level ITCMD at progressive rates up to 8% on distributions or transfers under Complementary Law 227/2026. The amnesty period for regularisation of offshore trust assets expired in 2024, making full disclosure mandatory.
**What it means for clients:** UHNW Brazilian clients with offshore trusts now face a combined effective rate that can reach 23% or more on trust-level income plus distributions, fundamentally changing the economics of common offshore succession structures. Existing structures should be reviewed for restructuring options or recalibration of distribution timing.
**Source:** ZS Advogados — 2026-07-01 — https://zsassociados.com/areas/services/trusts-in-brazil-guide/

### 👁 WATCH — LC 227/2026 mandates progressive ITCMD (up to 8%) on foreign inheritances, gifts, and trust distributions received by Brazilian residents
**What changed:** Complementary Law 227/2026 implements Constitutional Amendment 132/2023, requiring all Brazilian states to adopt progressive ITCMD rates (2%–8%) on assets inherited or gifted from abroad, including foreign trust distributions. Articles 146–151 specifically address trusts with multiple potential ITCMD trigger points: at creation, on annual earnings attribution, and on distribution — creating triple-trigger exposure.
**What it means for clients:** Brazilian-resident beneficiaries of foreign trusts now face ITCMD at progressive rates at each possible trigger event, not just on final distribution — meaning clients could incur ITCMD annually on attributed income in addition to federal CFC tax, with a further ITCMD hit at distribution. Trust deed review and state-by-state transition analysis is advisable for all Brazilian clients with foreign trust interests.
